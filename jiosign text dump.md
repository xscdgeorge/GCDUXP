# Winning back lost customers with a Mobile 1st experience & simplified pricing

### "Take my money" user after testing our AI feature in the prototype

**Situation**

I discovered the JioSign team, when scouting for other SaaS to build into Jio’s digital identity & governance ecosystem, alongside JioID.

The JioSign e-signature platform faced severe adoption issues internally & externally. Hundreds of internal businesses chose to pay millions for third-party services instead of utilizing the free in-house product. Critically, the platform’s mobile experience was unusable, resulting in a low **57% Task Completion Rate (TCR)** and **0 conversions**, which rendered the platform unviable for most businesses.

I convinced leadership that the project is better handled by my team which embeds designers into the team rather than provide an agency approach, as it requires a lot more ownership to influence decisions, and had the project transferred to my team.

**Task**  
My core task was to lead the design vision and strategy for JioSign 2.0. We had to transform JioSign into not only a viable, domain-focused freemium product, but a competitive one. This required my senior designer and I to do deep analysis of the existing product, the e-signature domain,customers, and prioritizing key areas for the MVP.

**Action**  
I conducted a few interviews with senior leaders and strategic customers. I facilitated multiple workshops to map the **Customer Journey**, breakdown the product through **Object-Oriented UX (OOUX).** It removed misconceptions, and aligned the cross-functional teams of Design, Product, Engineering, Business, internal customers, and subject matter experts. I guided the Sr. designer to refine her qualitative & quantitative research findings into actionable insights.

Actionable Insights

1. **Mobile specific experience is a must have, not a good to have:** Majority of signatories open the invite to sign on mobile from email or Whatsapp. Doc owners are often on the go from one client location to another. Without excellent support for mobile, Task Completion Rates will remain poor. Re-sharing links on Whatsapp is common practice as email & sms invites are ignored by non-business signatories, reinforcing mobile issues faced.  
2. **Signatories are intimidated by in-person agents rushing them to sign the doc**. Often sharing their personal mobile device with the agent to assist. Signatories who start-up their un-used laptops are bombarded with system dialogues and update requests that delay the process.  
3. **Assist with translations or summary:** Signatories often sign documents without understanding the jargon or language. Taking away the ambiguity would certainly give peace of mind as a quality of life feature.  
4. **Role based access and team experiences need to be built:** Password sharing is currently being used as a workaround to collaborate on documents. This is wrong on so many fronts, especially legally\!  
5. **Businesses need to create custom templates & workflows:** Without which they are stuck re-uploading the same docs and setting up workflows each time they need to send a document. Industry specific templates like HR, Logistics, Inventory, Legal, etc., will make adoption easier.  
6. **Upsell when a document is successfully signed:**  
   Without an upsell flow on successful signing we are loosing out on the best moment to convert a user to a business account. Touch-points with potential customers should not be wasted.  
7. **Provide modular integrations into legacy tooling, and related services** which cover their e2e CX like cloud storage, Aadhaar verification, DigiLocker, POS devices, etc: Otherwise we lose customers to competitors offering all-in-one solution, ID verifications, and SDK &/ API integrations. We can compete with 3rd party modules post customer acquisition, through pricing and seamless experiences.

We agreed on the scope & defined the MVP problem statements to 

1. How might we reduce friction to sign documents?  
2. How might we improve the doc setup, send and track experience? 

And agreed on a few Design Principles for our Product Led Growth approach. 

- Mobile 1st   
- Intuitive in India  
- Templates  
- Role-Based  
- Embeddable  
- Accessible  
- Fast

Key actions taken in the design and implementation of the mobile-first MVP:

* **Mobile-First Design Strategy:** Established the central design principle to provide an adaptive mobile-first experience, recognizing that the majority of signatories access invites via email or WhatsApp on their phones.  
* **Streamlined Authentication with JioID:** Replaced previous login methods by implementing **OTP (One-Time Password)** for both email and mobile number, which is intuitive in India.

* **Improved Signatory Flow:**  
  * Introduced clear instructions and videos to guide the user.  
  * Guides the user through required input fields (e.g., designation, age) **one-by-one** across the document to reduce friction.  
  * Allowed signatories to correct their name if misspelled by the document owner.  
  * Implemented strict signing order rules so participants only receive the invite link when the previous signatory has completed their task.

* **Document Owner Empowerment:**  
  * Enabled document owners to upload documents, rename, and change multi-document settings directly from their mobile phone.  
  * Simplified the process for rearranging the signing order on mobile.  
  * Added **WhatsApp support** for sharing the invite link, resolving the issue of users missing emails/SMS links.  
  * Introduced multi-select options for bulk actions (e.g., bulk sign, send reminders, delete) on the documents list page.  
* **AI Integration:** Ideated and scoped AI-enhanced features, including the option for the document owner to allow the AI to convert the document to mobile-friendly formats and legally **translate the document**.

**Results in Pilot**  
The introduction of the mobile-first experience and redesigned flows resulted in significant performance improvements:

* **Task Completion Rate (TCR) Improvement:** Improved the TCR by a massive **46%**.  
* **Efficiency:** During pilot tests, signatories achieved a **96% TCR** and were able to sign and submit documents in under **30 seconds** when using the option to apply signatures to all slots at once.  
* **User Satisfaction:**  
  * Signatories scored **74 SUS** and **8 NPS**.  
  * Document Owners scored **78 SUS** and **8.5 NPS**.  
* **Qualitative Feedback:** User validation was strong, with one test participant stating, **"Take my money"** after testing the AI translation feature prototype.
